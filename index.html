Welcome to the Bernardine Cistercian Sisters of Audenarde

For over 800 years, our Congregation has served God and humanity with love and dedication. Founded in 1202 in Belgium, we are committed to a life of prayer, simplicity, and service — working in education, healthcare, pastoral care, and rural development across Belgium, Rwanda, Tchad, and Burkina Faso. Guided by the Cistercian spirit of Saint Bernard, we seek God and embrace every person as His image.

The Bernardine Cistercian Sisters of Audenarde
1. Who are we?

The Congregation of the Bernardine Cistercian Sisters of Audenarde was founded in Audenarde, Belgium, around the year 1202. It currently has two provinces:

  • One in Belgium
  • Another in Africa, which includes Rwanda, Chad, and Burkina Faso.
2. Charism

"To seek God and welcome Man, made in the image of God"

4. Spirituality

The Congregation lives the Cistercian spirituality according to the spirit of Saint Bernard. This spirituality is an experience of seeking God, emphasizing:

  • Community life, called the “School of Charity”
  • Silence in prayer of the heart
  • The liturgy: the Eucharist, the Divine Office
  • The Word of God read and meditated upon (Lectio Divina)
  • Self-knowledge, leading to humility and ongoing conversion through charity
  • Simplicity and sobriety of life
  • Love of the Church under the guidance of the Virgin Mary, in the example of Saint Bernard

Our Missionary Presence Time line

• 1932

– Foundation of the first house in Rwanda: Notre Dame du Bon Conseil (Our Lady of Good Counsel).

• 1983

– Rwanda, in turn, became missionary by opening its first community in Chad.

• 1995

– Opening of a new missionary house in Bobo-Dioulasso, Burkina Faso

• 1995

– Second community in Chad (Bao): Our Lady of Peace.

• 2004

– Third community in Chad (Moundou): Mary, Mother of Mercy

Backgound of the foundation

The Congregation was founded by a priest (canon) named Arnulf, who wished to love and serve God in the poor. The house he built was first an inn for pilgrims who found themselves outside the closed city gates. Soon after, it became a hospital where the poor and needy were cared for — this was the Notre Dame Hospital of Oudenaarde.

This community distinguished itself by its fervor and dedication to the poor. Affiliated with Cîteaux, the young congregation took Saint Bernard as its spiritual father. After the French Revolution, the Congregation, which until then had been dedicated to hospital work, opened itself to education — especially for young girls, who were often neglected.

Later, it continued striving to respond to the new calls of the local and universal Church, as well as of society, while remaining attentive to the signs of the times.

Admission Requirements & Formation
Admission Requirements
  • A sincere desire to give oneself to God
  • An engaged Christian life
  • Be between 20 and 30 years of age
  • Prior general human and Christian formation, with great openness of mind to benefit effectively from religious life formation and to live well with others
  • Completion of high school or vocational training
  • At least 2 years of work or life experience after school
Formation of Young Candidates

Young women should contact the community closest to them for more detailed information about their vocation. Currently, the postulancy is carried out in the country of origin if there is already a community there. The novitiate takes place in Rwanda.

Duration of Initial Formation
  • Pre-postulancy: at least 1 year
  • Postulancy: 1 year
  • Canonical novitiate: 1 year
  • Community stage: 1 year
  • Preparation for vows: 6 months
